## What Happened

### Technology splits in two as ServiceNow cuts and Texas Instruments rips

A single session did more to reshape technology-sector positioning than any in recent weeks. ServiceNow (NOW), the enterprise workflow-software platform, fell 17.75% to $84.78 after trimming its full-year subscription adjusted gross-margin target to 31.5% from 32% and citing Middle-East conflict for on-premise deal slippage; Goldman Sachs, Jefferies, and Piper Sandler each cut price targets by more than 20%. The selling travelled: Workday (WDAY) fell 9.42%, Salesforce (CRM) 8.69%, IBM 8.25%, Palantir (PLTR) 7.24% on no company-specific news, and the SaaS cohort as a group lost roughly a year of valuation re-rating inside a day. Inside the same tape, Texas Instruments (TXN), the largest American analog-semiconductor manufacturer, rose 19.43% to $282.23 on a Q1 print that came in 7% above revenue consensus with Q2 guidance well ahead of the Street; sympathy carried Microchip Technology (MCHP) up 9.89%, ON Semiconductor 9.88%, and Analog Devices (ADI) 5.89%. The graph reads this as a day-one regime signal — the market has split technology into an AI-builder beneficiary cohort (analog, memory, foundry) and an AI-user/disruption-target cohort (horizontal SaaS, IT services), and is pricing each half with conviction. Tomorrow's tell is whether the SaaS rout keeps travelling to adjacent names (Accenture, already at a 52-week low, is the cleanest leading indicator) or cauterises with NOW. [CONFIRMED — NOW guidance 8-K, TXN IR release, 2026-04-23]

### Newmont prints a record quarter and doubles its buyback

Newmont Corporation (NEM), the world's largest gold miner, reported first-quarter 2026 results on Wednesday: revenue of $7.307bn (up 45.85% year-on-year), adjusted earnings of $2.90 per share, adjusted EBITDA of $5.2bn, and free cash flow of $3.1bn — a record on the final metric. All-in sustaining costs fell 29% year-on-year to $1,029 per ounce; realised gold averaged $4,900 per ounce against a full-year guide assumption of $4,500. The board doubled capital-return capacity with a new $6bn share-repurchase authorisation on top of the prior $6bn programme that was just exhausted, and declared a quarterly dividend of $0.26. Natascha Viljoen, Newmont's chief executive (having succeeded Tom Palmer), framed the buyback as a per-share-growth framework rather than a one-off return. Shares closed down 0.71% at $111.06, the precious complex broadly weak as the ten-year Treasury yield added 2.9 basis points — the tape refused to price the print, which is itself informative. The sustainability test runs through Ghana (where a new sliding royalty adds roughly $25 per ounce that is not in guidance) and Cadia (where a 4.5-magnitude earthquake on 14 April will cost a Q2 production gap before Q3 normalisation); the capital-allocation question is whether buyback pacing holds if gold reverts toward the $4,500 assumption. A deep-research evaluation of NEM is gated on transcript confirmation, which remains pending on day one. [CONFIRMED — NEM 10-Q 0001164727-26-000019; 8-K 0001164727-26-000017, filed 2026-04-23]

### Lockheed Martin's free-cash-flow turn gives the defence de-rate an operational leg

Lockheed Martin (LMT), the largest American defence prime, reported on Wednesday: revenue $18.0bn flat year-on-year, earnings of $6.44 per share against $7.28 in the prior-year quarter, cash from operations $220m against $1.4bn, and — the load-bearing number — free cash flow of negative $291m against positive $955m a year earlier. Management announced $7bn of PAC-3 orders, $2.2bn of which lands in 2026 deliveries. James Taiclet, chief executive since 2020, framed the quarter as programme-timing-driven, but the sequence now running across the primes is hard to read that way: Northrop Grumman (NOC) and Raytheon Technologies (RTX) printed clean beats into selling on Tuesday; Lockheed printed a weaker operational quarter on Wednesday; General Dynamics (GD) sits at a fresh 52-week low of $318.71 into its Q1 release next Tuesday. Shares rallied 16-20% after hours. The read-through to Micron Technology (MU), America's largest memory-chip manufacturer, is mechanical: Data Center and AI capital expenditure is the leading indicator for high-bandwidth-memory demand, and +22% growth adds to the sold-out-through-2027 chorus that already included SK Hynix's 62% HBM share commentary, TSMC's PT-raise tape, and Samsung's first HBM4 commercial shipments. ### SNAP package-food restrictions go live in eighteen states

The Florida Healthy SNAP programme took effect on 20 April, excluding soda, energy drinks, candy, and specifically-named ultra-processed shelf-stable desserts (Chips Ahoy, Oreos, Twinkies, Ho Hos, snack cakes) from Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Programme eligibility; seventeen other states are implementing parallel bans across 2026 under demonstration authority running through April 2028. The product-level mechanism is materially different from broad benefit cuts: it routes shopper spend toward fresh and private-label categories rather than reducing basket size. Direct read-through: General Mills (GIS), McCormick (MKC) and the packaged-food complex carry concentrated exposure; Dollar General (DG) and Dollar Tree (DLTR) both over-index on SNAP-payment share of baskets. The beneficiaries — Walmart (WMT), whose Great Value private-label refresh landed the same week, and off-price retailers with private-label capability — get a mechanism tailwind that compounds with the California Attorney-General's unsealed Amazon antitrust allegations, which implicate WMT, Home Depot, Target and Chewy in Amazon-induced pricing effects ahead of a January 2027 trial. Multi-quarter cascade rather than one-day event; edge weights on the packaged-foods-sector macro node stepped up accordingly. [CONFIRMED — Florida DCF / USDA FNS, 2026-04-20; REPORTED — California AG unsealing via Bloomberg]
